About

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais (UFMG), working in Differential Geometry under the supervision of Ezequiel Rodrigues Barbosa. My research focuses on the study of Weingarten surfaces and related problems in geometric analysis, including constant mean curvature surfaces and anisotropic curvature models.

I obtained my Master's degree in Mathematics from the Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais in 2024. My master's thesis, entitled Special Regular Algebraic Weingarten Surfaces in Euclidean Space, studied algebraic Weingarten surfaces of constant mean curvature type in ℝ³.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from the Universidade Federal de Viçosa (UFV – Florestal). During my undergraduate studies I worked on topics related to geodesics and Riemannian geometry, including a project on geodesic flows and a monograph on the Hopf–Rinow theorem.

My academic interests include:

  • Differential Geometry
  • Weingarten surfaces
  • Constant mean curvature surfaces
  • Anisotropic mean curvature
  • Geometric analysis

I have presented research at several mathematical meetings, including the Colóquio Brasileiro de Matemática and the São Paulo Meeting on Geometry.
