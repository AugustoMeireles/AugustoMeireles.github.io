About

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} (UFMG), working in Differential Geometry under the supervision of :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}. My research focuses on the study of Weingarten surfaces and related problems in geometric analysis, including constant mean curvature surfaces and anisotropic curvature models.

I obtained my Master's degree in Mathematics from :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2} in 2024. My master's thesis, entitled Special Regular Algebraic Weingarten Surfaces in Euclidean Space, studied algebraic Weingarten surfaces of constant mean curvature type in ℝ³.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3} (UFV – Florestal). During my undergraduate studies I worked on topics related to geodesics and Riemannian geometry, including a project on geodesic flows and a monograph on the Hopf–Rinow theorem.

My academic interests include:

  • Differential Geometry
  • Weingarten surfaces
  • Constant mean curvature surfaces
  • Anisotropic mean curvature
  • Geometric analysis

I have presented research at several mathematical meetings, including the :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4} and the São Paulo Meeting on Geometry.
